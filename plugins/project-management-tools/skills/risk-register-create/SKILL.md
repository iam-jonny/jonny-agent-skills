# Risk Register Create

## Shared Reference

Use `../../references/project-management-principles.md` for general project
management principles. Apply the risk and issue management, planning, status,
and change control sections.

## Risk Stance

Separate risks from issues. A risk might happen and needs monitoring or
mitigation. An issue is already happening and needs action. Make owners,
triggers, and escalation thresholds explicit.

## Workflow

1. Extract risks, issues, assumptions, blockers, dependencies, and stakeholder
   concerns.
2. Classify each item as risk, issue, assumption, or dependency.
3. Estimate impact, likelihood, urgency, and confidence.
4. Assign owner, mitigation, contingency, trigger, and escalation threshold.
5. Identify which risks should appear in status reports or sponsor updates.

## Output

Return:

- Risk register table.
- Issue log table when current issues exist.
- Top risks requiring sponsor attention.
- Mitigation and contingency plan.
- Escalation thresholds.
- Monitoring cadence.
- Open information needed.

## Common Failure Modes

- Listing risks without owners.
- Treating current issues as future risks.
- No trigger for when to escalate.
- Mitigation actions that are vague or unactionable.
- Risk register disconnected from status reporting.

## Quality Bar

The risk register should help the project team reduce uncertainty and know
when to act, escalate, or change the plan.
