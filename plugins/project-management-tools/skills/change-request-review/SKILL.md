# Change Request Review

## Shared Reference

Use `../../references/project-management-principles.md` for general project
management principles. Apply change control, stakeholder communication, risk,
and planning sections.

## Review Stance

A useful change can still be the wrong change now. Evaluate the tradeoff
against project objectives, constraints, risks, and decision rights. Do not
approve changes without making the impact explicit.

## Workflow

1. Identify requested change, requester, reason, and decision owner.
2. Assess impact on scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, dependencies,
   stakeholder commitments, and communication.
3. Identify alternatives: reject, defer, split, substitute, or approve with
   conditions.
4. Recommend decision and approval path.
5. Draft decision log and stakeholder message.

## Output

Return:

- Change summary.
- Impact assessment.
- Options considered.
- Recommendation: `Approve`, `Approve with conditions`, `Reject`, `Defer`,
  `Split`, or `Escalate`.
- Decision owner and approvers.
- Approval conditions.
- Required plan, risk, or communication updates.
- Decision log entry.
- Stakeholder message draft.

## Common Failure Modes

- Evaluating change only by value, not tradeoff.
- No decision owner.
- Impact on dependencies or communication is missed.
- Approved change does not update plan or status.
- Rejecting a change without explaining alternatives.

## Quality Bar

The review should make the decision defensible and show what must change in the
project plan if the request is approved.
