# Business Requirements Review

## Shared Reference

Use `../../references/product-management-principles.md` for general product
management principles. Apply the discovery, metrics, prioritization, and common
failure mode sections when reviewing business requirements.

## Review Stance

Treat business requirements as the contract between strategy and product execution. Push on outcomes, economics, constraints, and stakeholder alignment before solution details.

## Product Management Principles

- A business requirement should justify why product capacity should be spent
  now.
- The requirement must connect customer or market pain to a measurable business
  outcome.
- Solution preferences are useful context, but should not replace problem,
  segment, value, constraints, and success criteria.
- Good business requirements make the decision owner, impacted teams, and
  approval path visible.

## Practical Operating Guidance

- Check whether the initiative has a named accountable owner and approvers.
- Look for budget, timing, legal, operational, technical, and GTM constraints
  before judging feasibility.
- Ask what decision the document should unlock: PRD creation, funding,
  roadmap placement, experiment approval, or cancellation.
- Flag any requirement that cannot be tied to revenue, cost, risk, retention,
  adoption, quality, strategic positioning, or learning.
- Treat "important stakeholder asked for it" as evidence to inspect, not as a
  sufficient business case.

## Checklist

- Business objective: clear outcome, not only output.
- Customer or user impact: target segment and pain are explicit.
- Success metrics: measurable, attributable, and time-bounded.
- Scope: in-scope, out-of-scope, and non-goals are stated.
- Constraints: legal, operational, technical, budget, timing, and go-to-market constraints are visible.
- Stakeholders: owners, approvers, impacted teams, and unresolved decisions are named.
- Risks: adoption, revenue, cost, compliance, delivery, and support risks are addressed.

## Common Failure Modes

- The document asks for a feature but does not state the business problem.
- Success metrics are vague, lagging only, or not attributable to the work.
- Scope boundaries are implied rather than explicit.
- Stakeholders are named, but decision rights are unclear.
- Constraints appear late, after product or engineering has already committed.

## Output

Provide findings first, ordered by severity. Then list missing decisions, suggested requirement rewrites, and a readiness verdict: `Ready for PRD`, `Needs clarification`, or `Not ready`.
