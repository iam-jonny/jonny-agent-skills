# MVV Review

## Shared Reference

Use `../../references/product-management-principles.md` for general product
management principles. Apply the outcome, tradeoff, and common failure mode
sections when testing whether the MVV can guide decisions.

## Review Stance

Evaluate whether the MVV can actually guide decisions. Be direct about vague, interchangeable, or performative language.

## Product Management Principles

- Mission should identify the beneficiary, purpose, and durable job.
- Vision should describe a distinct future state, not a near-term delivery
  plan.
- Values should be behavioral standards that influence decisions under
  pressure.
- Strong MVV language creates useful exclusion: it clarifies what the team will
  not do.

## Practical Operating Guidance

- Test whether the MVV can decide between two competing roadmap options.
- Replace generic words with concrete beneficiaries, behaviors, and tradeoffs.
- Flag values that cannot be observed in product, hiring, customer support,
  brand, or operating decisions.
- If the MVV is too broad, ask which audience or market the organization is
  willing to serve better than others.

## Checklist

- Mission: names the beneficiary, purpose, and enduring job.
- Vision: describes a distinct future state, not a near-term milestone.
- Values: describe observable behaviors and tradeoffs.
- Coherence: mission, vision, and values reinforce each other.
- Differentiation: language would not fit any random competitor.
- Usability: teams can use it to resolve product, hiring, brand, or prioritization decisions.

## Common Failure Modes

- Mission names the company but not the beneficiary.
- Vision describes being "leading" or "trusted" without a specific future.
- Values are nouns instead of behaviors.
- The artifact avoids the organization's real strategic tension.
- The words are memorable but not decision-useful.

## Output

Lead with findings ordered by severity. For each finding, include the issue, why it matters, and a concrete rewrite or decision question. End with a tightened draft only when enough context exists.
