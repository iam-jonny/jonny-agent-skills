# Roadmap Create

## Shared Reference

Use `../../references/product-management-principles.md` for general product
management principles. Apply the prioritization, metrics, roadmap, and common
failure mode sections when drafting roadmaps.

## Workflow

1. Confirm the roadmap audience and horizon: leadership, product team, engineering, GTM, customers, or board.
2. Anchor the roadmap in outcomes and strategic themes before listing features.
3. Group work into now/next/later, quarters, milestones, or bets based on the user's planning style.
4. Show dependencies, confidence, risks, and decision points.
5. Avoid over-committing exact dates unless the user provides delivery constraints.

## Product Management Principles

- A roadmap explains strategy, sequencing, confidence, and tradeoffs.
- Roadmaps should organize work around outcomes and themes before features.
- Time horizons should reflect confidence: closer work can be more specific,
  later work should preserve optionality.
- Committed, planned, exploratory, and dependent work should be clearly
  distinguishable.

## Practical Operating Guidance

- Identify the audience first; leadership, engineering, GTM, customers, and
  board audiences need different detail.
- Sequence work by value, dependency, risk reduction, learning, capacity, and
  market timing.
- Include non-feature work such as platform, migration, quality, security,
  compliance, instrumentation, enablement, and support readiness.
- Add decision checkpoints where future work depends on evidence or delivery
  outcomes.
- State confidence levels and what would change the roadmap.

## Output

Return a roadmap with:

- Strategic themes
- Outcome targets
- Initiatives or bets
- Time horizon or sequencing
- Dependencies and constraints
- Risks and assumptions
- Decision checkpoints

## Common Failure Modes

- The roadmap becomes a feature calendar with no strategic logic.
- Dates imply certainty the team does not have.
- Dependencies and capacity constraints are hidden.
- Platform, quality, migration, compliance, and support work are invisible.
- Different audiences cannot tell what is committed versus exploratory.

## Quality Bar

Make the roadmap useful for tradeoffs. It should explain why work is sequenced, what can move, and what success means.
